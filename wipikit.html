You already decided
you don’t need it.

It’s been sitting there for weeks. Maybe months. Moved from one place to another, but never used.

Not important enough to keep.
Not urgent enough to let go.

So it stays.

Let it go

We’ll come by. Usually the same or next day.

What tends to stay

Clothes you don’t reach for
Bags that never leave the shelf
Sunglasses you forgot you had
Small electronics in drawers
Shoes you meant to fix
Things that almost matter

What happens next

You pick a time
We come to your door
It leaves your space — and keeps moving

Why this works

Most things don’t leave because they’re valuable. They stay because they’re undecided.

Once they’re gone, the space feels different. Lighter, quieter — easier to keep that way.
